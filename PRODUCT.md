# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Pocket Closet is for household members who manage clothing across kids, adults, closets, dressers, bins, donate piles, and seasonal storage. The primary early user is a parent adding and finding children's clothing quickly while dealing with overflow and future sizes.

## Product Purpose

Pocket Closet is a local-first iOS clothing inventory app. It helps a household capture individual clothing items with photos and structured metadata, find what is available by owner/type/size/location/status, and decide what should stay, move, sell, donate, or be archived.

Success means a user can add an item in under 20 seconds after defaults are set, filter hundreds of local items instantly, and answer practical questions like "what do we have for Emma in 4T?" without digging through storage.

## Brand Personality

Calm, practical, and warm. The app should feel like a native iOS household utility with enough polish to be pleasant, not like an ecommerce storefront or a heavy database tool.

## Anti-references

Do not make the app feel like a marketing landing page, a custom ecommerce catalog, a kid-only app, or a highly decorative Dribbble mockup. Avoid forced gendering, birthday-driven assumptions, AI-first capture, and heavy visual effects that slow down entry.

## Design Principles

- Photo first: clothing images are the fastest recognition layer.
- Fast capture wins: preserve useful defaults and keep required metadata focused.
- Location is first-class: inventory is only useful when the user knows where the item is.
- Structured where it matters: use real US sizes instead of freeform primary sizing.
- Native over novel: lean on familiar iOS navigation, lists, sheets, pickers, and accessibility behavior.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Support Dynamic Type, VoiceOver labels, reduced motion, dark mode, and sufficient color contrast. Color must never be the only way to understand owner, status, or selected filter state.
